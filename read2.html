What Does the Term 'Integrated Air Ambulance' Refer To?

An integrated air ambulance is a category of air ambulance that offers both medical care and transportation services to patients..

The concept of an integrated air ambulance was initially introduced in the 1990s, recognizing the limitation of transporting critically injured patients solely by helicopter.

Due to their high cost, helicopters are reserved for situations where no alternative is available. Nevertheless, the incorporation of air ambulances allows for the transportation of patients via land, sea, or even road, enhancing accessibility, especially for individuals residing in remote areas.

Can Life Flight fly at night?

What is the Capacity of Patients an Air Ambulance Can Transport?

The operation of air ambulances in the United States is overseen by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). The FAA's Air Medical Transport Advisory Committee (AMTAC) provides recommendations regarding the maximum patient capacity for air ambulances.

The FAA has set a maximum weight limit of 450 pounds per individual for each flight; however, this criterion may not always be adhered to, as some patients exceed the 450-pound limit, while others weigh less than 450 pounds.

Who Travels in An Air Ambulance?

Air ambulances transport patients who need immediate medical care or surgery.

Typically, these air ambulances are staffed with a doctor, nurse, and paramedic. The patient is positioned on a stretcher that has the capability to be elevated and lowered within the ambulance.
